[Ego surfing](http://www.microcontentnews.com/resources/glossary/egosurfing.htm) is an honorable tradition; punch your name (or affiliation) into Google, and see what people are saying about you. We’ve all done it. However, it never occurred that [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com) with its new full text index, was ready and primed for ego surfing.

Until this morning that is, when we got an email from [Inner Ocean](http://innerocean.com), the [publisher](http://www.innerocean.com/news.htm?a=33430&c=1) of [MoveOn’s](http://moveon.org) new book [50 Ways to Love Your Country](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/193072229X/002-0743474-8786455?v=glance). (with apologies to Paul Simon?) Apparently [Protst.net](http://protest.net) is mentioned in the book, and they’ll be sending us a complimentary copy.(and we decided probably made better sense to tell them to ship it to Seattle rather then [Rome](http://www.anarchogeek.com/archives/000334.html))

Now alive to possibility, I went and checked. Turns out Amazon shows [11 books that mention Protest.net](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/search-handle-url/index%3Dstripbooks%26field-keywords%3D%2522protest.net%2522/002-0743474-8786455). (plus a few false positives, and no sign of the MovOn book)
