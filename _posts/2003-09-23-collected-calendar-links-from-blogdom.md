### aka I love Feedster

I’m not really able to keep up with blogdom like I used to. Which is why I love [Feedster](http://feedster.com). Collected links of the recent discussion of iCalendar, RSS, and events.

David Galbraith speculates about how to [patch event info into RSS 0.92 using structured URLs.](http://www.davidgalbraith.org/archives/000476.html) I like structured URLs, but why not use an RSS format that supports namespaces?

Marc Canter [draws a picture of a “next generation event aggregator”](http://blogs.it/0100198/2003/09/22.html#a1744) mediating between websites, and traditional event stores..

[Blog as .ics file redux](http://theorganization.net/mt/archives/2003/09/22/ical_and_blogging.html).

Nick has a cute [event widget mockup](http://radio.weblogs.com/0110130/2003/09/22.html#a652)

Surfmind is maintaining an [RSS+event feed](http://www.surfmind.com/rlc/calendar/rss.cfm) for the [Red Light Cafe](http://www.redlightcafe.com/) and musing about [next steps and MozCal integration](http://www.surfmind.com/musings/2003/09/22/).

Libby proposes a dencentralized [Upcoming](http://upcoming.org) based on [ical, foaf and movable type](http://planb.nicecupoftea.org/archives/000068.html).

NewsGoat is publishing the [OK Mensa calendar](http://www.okmensa.org/calendar/) with [Moveable Type and Brad Choate style magic](http://www.newsgoat.com/2003/09/10/1725/).

Also the W3C roadshow is available [as an .ics file](http://www.w3.org/Promotion/Appearances/cal.ics).

Jon is playing with [prying information out of Outlook](http://weblog.infoworld.com/udell/2003/09/23.html#a803).

Element12 is [wondering why there has been no effective implementation of an “add this to my diary”.](http://www.element12.net/darwin/archives/000041.html)
